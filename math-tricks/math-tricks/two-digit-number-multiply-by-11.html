Vedic Math: Quick Multiplication by 11

Explanation:

To multiply a two-digit number by 11, follow these steps:

  • Write down the first and last digit.
  • Add the two digits together.
  • Place the sum in the middle.
  • If the sum is 10 or more, carry over the extra 1.

Examples with Explanation:

Example 1: 47 × 11

Step 1: First digit = 4, Last digit = 7

Step 2: Add 4 + 7 = 11

Step 3: Place sum in the middle, but since it's 11, we carry over 1.

Final Answer: 517

Example 2: 63 × 11

Step 1: First digit = 6, Last digit = 3

Step 2: Add 6 + 3 = 9

Step 3: Place 9 in the middle.

Final Answer: 693

Example 3: 85 × 11

Step 1: First digit = 8, Last digit = 5

Step 2: Add 8 + 5 = 13

Step 3: Place 3 in the middle and carry over 1.

Final Answer: 935

Example 4: 29 × 11

Step 1: First digit = 2, Last digit = 9

Step 2: Add 2 + 9 = 11

Step 3: Place 1 in the middle and carry over 1.

Final Answer: 319

Example 5: 38 × 11

Step 1: First digit = 3, Last digit = 8

Step 2: Add 3 + 8 = 11

Step 3: Place 1 in the middle and carry over 1.

Final Answer: 418
